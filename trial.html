❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your pet's first visit — and beyond.

For healthy adult dogs and cats, we recommend a comprehensive wellness exam once a year. Senior pets (7+ years) benefit from bi-annual checkups to catch age-related conditions early. Puppies and kittens need exams more frequently in their first year to complete vaccine series and monitor development.
Core vaccines for dogs include Distemper, Parvovirus, Adenovirus, and Rabies. For cats, we recommend Feline Herpesvirus, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia, and Rabies. Non-core vaccines (like Bordetella, Leptospirosis, and Lyme) are recommended based on your pet's lifestyle and risk factors. Our vets will create a personalised vaccine schedule at your first visit.
Yes. PawCare operates a 24/7 emergency line at +1 (999) 555-0911. Our emergency team is always on call for life-threatening situations including difficulty breathing, severe trauma, ingestion of toxins, uncontrolled bleeding, and seizures. Please call ahead so we can prepare for your arrival.
PawCare treats a wide range of companion animals including dogs, cats, rabbits, guinea pigs, birds, reptiles (lizards, snakes, turtles), small rodents, and fish. Some exotic species may require a referral to a specialist — please call us in advance if you're unsure whether we see your specific pet.
You can book an appointment through our online form on this page, by calling us at +1 (555) 234-5678, or by emailing appointments@pawcarevc.com. We typically confirm bookings within 2 hours during business hours. Walk-ins are also welcome for non-emergency visits, subject to availability.
Yes! PawCare works with all major pet insurance providers. We provide detailed itemised invoices and medical records to support your insurance claims. We can also help you navigate your policy and advise on which coverage options best suit your pet's needs if you're considering pet insurance for the first time.
